Engaging Youth to Improve Public Services

Government departments across Pakistan face challenges in delivering efficient, transparent, and citizen-friendly services. The Civic Innovator Program (CIP) by Code for Pakistan connects final-year university students with government institutions and industry mentors to build digital tools that enhance public services.

Through iteration, process improvements, and open-source civic technology, CIP helps make governance more accessible, accountable, and effective.

HOW IT WORKS

CIP runs over one academic year (7th & 8th semester). A steering committee with representatives from government, academia, and industry provides oversight twice a year.

Governments Submit Challenges

Identify pressing public service issues. Gain low-cost, scalable solutions to improve efficiency and citizen engagement.

Submit a Challenge
Universities Select Student Teams

Final-year students research, prototype, and develop solutions. Integrated into final-year coursework for academic rigor and faculty support.

Participate in CIP
Industry Mentors Provide Expertise

Guide students through mentorship and technical feedback. Ensure projects align with real-world needs and best practices.

Become a Mentor
Solutions Are Deployed

Tested and refined solutions are handed off to government partners. Projects remain open-source for wider adoption.

PROGRAM TIMELINE

The program runs for two academic semesters (7th & 8th), approximately one year, allowing sufficient time for in-depth development and collaboration on projects.
